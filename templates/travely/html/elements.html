  • Good vibes versus good skiing
    How Does Our Energy Affect Our Performance?
    December 7, 2014
  • It's interesting how people's energy levels and attitudes can be so easily affected by weather conditions. A few epic days of fresh powder will have everyone brimming with stoke and smiling from ear to ear. But when Mother Nature doesn't feel like bestowing a fresh dumping of snow upon us, many people often choose not to ski or snowboard and instead lament about less than favourable conditions. Sure, we'd all much prefer to be riding powder, but I think it's really important to remember that you can't have perfection all the time and you need to make the most of whatever you've got to work with.

  • OUR HISTRORY
    FROM FISHING TO FREE SKIING
  • It was fishing that drew the first tourists to YOUR RESORT NAME - to Rainbow Lodge on Alta Lake to cast for trout with legendary guides Alex and Myrtle Stones. By the early 1920s, Rainbow Lodge was the most popular summer destination west of the Rocky Mountains.

    Then winter hit. In 1960 a group of Vancv businessmen, led by Franz Wilhelmtinstil, formed Garibaldi Parts Limited with the aim of developing an alpine ski area on London Mountain (across the lake from Rainbow Lodge).         		Their dream: to host the 1968 Olympic Games. While it would take another 50 years and four Olympic bids before Whistler would realize its Olympic dream, Wilhelmsen and his cohorts pursued their ski area plans with great vigour.

    And so it was that London Mountain was soon renamed Whistler Mountain (in honour of a local alpine marmot, who "whistles" when it communicates) and officially opened to the public in February 1966 at the current THE RESORT NAME base.
    Boasting the biggest vertical drop in all of North America and that stretched from early November until late May, Whistler Mountain opened with a cool four-person gondola, a double chairlift, two T-bars, and a day lodge, and virtually re-invented the modern ski experience.
